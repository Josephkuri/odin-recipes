Ugali and Beef Stew Recipe

Ugali and Beef is a quintessential East African dish, cherished for its comforting simplicity and robust flavors. The dish combines two main components: Ugali, a staple made from maize flour and water, cooked to a thick, hearty consistency; and Beef Stew, featuring tender chunks of beef simmered in a savory blend of onions, tomatoes, garlic, and aromatic spices like curry powder and paprika.

The slow-cooking process ensures the meat is succulent and infused with rich flavors, creating a delicious contrast to the subtly sweet and satisfying Ugali. Garnished with fresh herbs, this dish is a wholesome meal that brings together the heartiness of African cuisine with an inviting, communal dining experience.

Ingredients

For Ugali

  • 400 grams maize flour (also known as cornmeal)
  • 1 liter water
  • Salt to taste

    • For Beef Stew

    • 500 g beef stew meat, cubed
    • 30 ml cooking oil
    • 1 onion, finely chopped
    • 2 tomatoes, chopped
    • 2 cloves garlic, minced
    • 10 grams ginger, minced
    • 5 grams curry powder
    • 5 grams paprika
    • 2 grams cumin powder
    • Salt and pepper to taste
    • 500 ml beef broth or water
    • Chopped fresh cilantro or parsley for garnish (optional)

    Steps

    1. Prepare the Ugali

    1. In a large saucepan, bring 1 liter of water to a boil.
    2. Gradually add the maize flour while stirring continuously to prevent lumps from forming.
    3. Reduce the heat to low and continue stirring until the mixture thickens and starts to pull away from the sides of the pan, about 10-15 minutes.
    4. Cover the saucepan with a lid and let the Ugali steam for another 5-10 minutes to cook through.
    5. Remove from heat and let it sit, covered, while you prepare the beef stew.

    1. Make the Beef Stew

      1. Heat the cooking oil in a large skillet or pot over medium-high heat.
      2. Add the onions and saut√© until they become translucent, about 3-4 minutes.
      3. Add the minced garlic and ginger, and cook for another 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
      4. Add the cubed beef to the skillet and brown on all sides, about 5-7 minutes.
      5. Stir in the chopped tomatoes, curry powder, paprika, cumin powder, salt, and pepper. Cook for 3-4 minutes until the tomatoes soften.
      6. Pour in the beef broth or water, bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover and simmer for about 1 to 1.5 hours, or until the beef is tender and the sauce has thickened. Stir occasionally and add more water if needed to prevent sticking.

        Serve

      1. To serve, scoop a portion of Ugali onto a plate or bowl.
      2. Ladle the beef stew over the Ugali.
      3. Garnish with chopped fresh cilantro or parsley if desired.
